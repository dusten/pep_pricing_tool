## N-Acetyl Epitalon Amidate vs Epitalon (Epithalon/AEDG) Comparison

| Aspect | Epitalon (Base) | N-Acetyl Epitalon Amidate (Ac-AEDG-NH₂) | Notes / Implications |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **CAS Number** | 307297-39-8 (primary) | N/A (not assigned; some vendors reuse base CAS) | Typical for custom research peptides. |
| **Molecular Formula** | C₁₄H₂₂N₄O₉ | C₁₆H₂₅N₅O₉ (free base) | Modifications add acetyl (C₂H₂O) and adjust amidation. |
| **Molecular Weight** | ~390.35 g/mol | ~431.4 g/mol (commonly 431.40–432.41) | Net +~41 Da from modifications. |
| **Sequence** | Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly (H-AEDG-OH) | Ac-Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly-NH₂ | Core active sequence identical. |
| **Modifications** | None | N-terminal acetylation + C-terminal amidation | Dual protection for stability. |
| **Metabolic Stability** | Lower; rapid degradation by exopeptidases | Significantly higher | Modified resists enzymatic breakdown better. |
| **Plasma Half-Life** | Short (typically minutes) | Extended | Better systemic exposure for modified form. |
| **Bioavailability** | Low | Improved | Lower doses often sufficient for modified. |
| **Storage (Reconstituted)** | Standard; degrades faster | Superior in solution | Vials last longer post-reconstitution. |
| **Typical Research Dosing** | 5–10 mg/day (higher total) | 100–1,000 mcg/day (lower total) | Efficiency advantage for modified. |
| **Research Backing** | Extensive (literature primarily on base) | Limited direct studies; inferred benefits | Base for protocol fidelity; modified for practicality. |
| **Mechanism of Action** | Telomerase upregulation, pineal/melatonin, antioxidant, gene expression | Identical core mechanism | Same biology, better delivery. |
| **Cost per mg** | Lower | Higher | Often comparable effective cost. |
| **Solubility** | Generally good | Variable (batch-dependent) | Gentle technique recommended. |
| **Best For** | Matching published studies | Practical stability, efficiency, handling | Choose based on priorities. |
