### Compounds Discussed

- **Thymosin B4 Acetate (Full / TB4)**: Natural 43-amino-acid peptide (CAS 77591-33-4). Broad regenerative, actin-sequestering, and immune-modulating effects.
- **Thymosin B5 Acetate / TB-500 Fragment**: Synthetic 7-amino-acid active core of TB4 (Ac-LKKTETQ, CAS 885340-08-9). Focused on localized tissue repair and cell migration.
- **TB5 (CAS 948841-07-4)**: Small-molecule MAO-B inhibitor (structure: (E)-1-(5-Bromothiophen-2-yl)-3-\[4-(dimethylamino)phenyl\]prop-2-en-1-one). **Not a peptide**. Used in neuroscience and dopamine regulation research.

### Key Evaluation Points

- **Nomenclature Confusion**: In peptide vendor communities, “Thymosin B5” or “TB-500 (B5)” almost always refers to the 7-aa synthetic fragment (CAS 885340-08-9), **not** the MAO-B inhibitor.
- **Vendor Error Identified**: One source (Biosynth) incorrectly described the MAO-B inhibitor (CAS 948841-07-4) as a “synthetic peptide derived from Thymosin Beta-4.” This is inaccurate — it is a small molecule.
- **CAS Verification**: All three CAS numbers are confirmed across multiple reputable chemical databases (PubChem, ChemicalBook, Cayman Chemical, Sigma-Aldrich, Wikipedia, etc.).
- **Type Difference**: B4 and B5 (peptide) are peptides. TB5 (948841-07-4) is a small molecule with a completely different mechanism (MAO-B inhibition).
- **Data Quality**:
	- Peptide data (B4 & B5) has strong preclinical support for tissue repair and regeneration.
		- MAO-B inhibitor data is primarily from neuroscience research; human clinical data is limited or absent for most applications discussed.
- **Stability & Practicality**: The 7-aa fragment (B5) is generally preferred in research for better stability and tissue penetration compared to the full 43-aa B4.
- **Research Context**: All compounds are for research use only. Human dosing, safety, and efficacy data are limited, especially for the fragment and MAO-B inhibitor.
