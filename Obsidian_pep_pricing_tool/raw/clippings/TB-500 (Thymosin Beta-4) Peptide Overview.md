## Peptide Overview

TB-500, scientifically designated as N-Acetyl-Leu-Lys-Lys-Thr-Glu-Thr-Gln or the actin-binding fragment (17-23) of thymosin beta-4 (Tβ4), is a synthetic eight-amino-acid peptide derived from the naturally occurring 43-amino-acid thymosin beta-4 protein found ubiquitously in human and animal cells. The peptide was engineered by isolating the core bioactive region responsible for actin binding and cell migration, creating a shorter, more stable synthetic version suitable for subcutaneous or intramuscular injection while maintaining the regenerative properties of the parent molecule.

TB-500 operates through multiple complementary mechanisms: (1) actin sequestration and regulation, promoting cell migration to injury sites through modification of the cytoskeletal protein actin (which comprises ~10% of total cellular proteins); (2) angiogenesis activation via Notch and NF-κB signaling pathway modulation, promoting new blood vessel formation from existing vessels; (3) endothelial cell differentiation and keratinocyte migration enhancement; and (4) anti-inflammatory effects through downregulation of pro-inflammatory cytokines and upregulation of anti-inflammatory signals. TB-500 also promotes extracellular matrix remodeling through increased collagen deposition and matrix metalloproteinase expression while simultaneously inhibiting fibrosis and scar formation.

Preclinical research in rodent injury models documents that TB-500 accelerates wound healing by 42-61% compared to controls, enhances muscle and tendon tissue regeneration, reduces inflammatory responses, prevents adhesion formation, improves joint mobility and flexibility through reduced fibrosis, supports angiogenesis in ischemic tissue, and promotes functional recovery from acute trauma—effects achieved through systemic administration without the androgenic or hormonal side effects associated with traditional anabolic agents.

## Chemical Identification

**Common Names:** TB-500, TB500, Thymosin Beta-4 Fragment, Thymosin B4 Acetate, Tβ4 Fragment, Actin-Binding Peptide, Tesamorelin

**CAS Number:** 885340-08-9 (TB-500 fragment free base); 77591-33-4 (full-length thymosin beta-4)

**Molecular Formula:** C₃₈H₆₈N₁₀O₁₄ (TB-500 fragment); C₂₁₂H₃₅₀N₅₆O₇₈S (full thymosin beta-4)

**Molecular Weight:** 889.02 g/mol (TB-500 fragment); 4963.44 g/mol (full thymosin beta-4)

**Origin & Type Classification:**

- **Source:** Synthetic; derived from naturally occurring thymosin beta-4
- **Biosynthesis:** Non-ribosomal; chemically synthesized via solid-phase peptide synthesis (SPPS) with N-terminal acetylation
- **Functional Class:** Actin-binding peptide; wound healing factor; angiogenic agent; tissue regeneration promoter; anti-inflammatory agent

**Additional Information:**

- **Amino Acid Sequence:** Ac-Leu-Lys-Lys-Thr-Glu-Thr-Gln (N-terminal acetyl group, eight amino acids: leucine, lysine, lysine, threonine, glutamic acid, threonine, glutamine)
- **Sequence Abbreviation:** Ac-LKKTETQ
- **Sequence Length:** 8 amino acids (octapeptide fragment)
- **Structural Type:** Linear octapeptide with N-terminal acetylation; free C-terminal carboxylic acid
- **Actin-Binding Domain:** Corresponds to amino acid positions 17-23 of full thymosin beta-4 (the core actin-binding region)
- **Actin Interaction:** Binds to globular actin (G-actin), sequestering it and preventing actin polymerization into filaments (F-actin)
- **Cell Migration Promotion:** Regulates cytoskeletal dynamics to enable rapid cell movement toward injury sites
- **Angiogenic Pathway Activation:** Stimulates Notch and NF-κB signaling cascades promoting angiogenesis
- **VEGF and Angiopoietin Effects:** Increases vascular endothelial growth factor A (VEGFA), angiopoietin-2, and Tie2 receptor expression
- **MicroRNA-146a Upregulation:** Suppresses TLR proinflammatory signaling through miR-146a elevation
- **Key Amino Acid Features:** Two lysine residues (positively charged, positions 2-3) enable receptor interactions; leucine (hydrophobic, N-terminus); glutamic acid (negatively charged); balanced polarity enabling both cell membrane interactions and actin binding
- **Physical Form:** White to off-white lyophilized powder
- **Salt Form:** Available as free base, acetate salt, or other salt forms; acetate form most commercially common
- **Water Solubility:** Excellent water solubility—50-250 mg/mL in sterile water depending on formulation
- **Storage:** Store at -20°C or below; protect from light; typically stable 6-12 months
- **Known Synonyms:** Thymosin beta-4 LKKTETQ, Actin sequestering peptide, Wound healing factor peptide, Recovery peptide
- **Supplier Identification:** PubChem CID available (related compounds); UNII (FDA identifier) QHK6Z47GTG

**Database Links:**

- **PubChem:** Full thymosin beta-4 (CID 16132341); TB-500 fragment referenced in chemical literature
- **UniProt:** Thymosin beta-4 (P62328); TB-500 as synthetic fragment not independently catalogued
- **PDB:** Not applicable
- **NCBI:** Extensive literature on thymosin beta-4 biology and wound healing

**Important Note:** TB-500 represents the core bioactive fragment of thymosin beta-4; full-length Tβ4 (43 amino acids) and TB-500 (8 amino acids) show comparable efficacy in many applications.

## Research Findings

**Wound Healing and Tissue Regeneration Acceleration**

**42-61% Reepithelialization Enhancement (Rat Full-Thickness Wound Model) ([PMID: 10469335](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10469335/))**

- **Study ([PMID: 10469335](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10469335/), cited in 1435, 1446):** Rat full-thickness wound model; topical or intraperitoneal thymosin beta-4 administration; measurement of reepithelialization rate and wound healing parameters at days 4 and 7 post-wounding.
	- **Findings:** Thymosin beta-4 increased reepithelialization 42% over saline controls at 4 days and 61% at 7 days. Treated wounds contracted 11% more by day 7. Increased collagen deposition and angiogenesis observed in treated wounds.

**Keratinocyte Migration Stimulation (2-3-Fold Increase)**

- **Study ([PMID: 10469335](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10469335/), cited in 1435, 1446):** Boyden chamber keratinocyte migration assay; measurement of cell migration with 10 pg thymosin beta-4.
	- **Findings:** Thymosin beta-4 stimulated keratinocyte migration 2-3-fold over control conditions after 4-5 hours, demonstrating potent epithelial cell mobilization.

**Angiogenesis and Blood Vessel Formation**

**VEGFA, Angiopoietin-2, and Tie2 Receptor Upregulation (PMID: cited in 1437)**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1437):** Investigation of TB-500 effects on angiogenic protein expression; measurement of VEGFA, angiopoietin-2, and Tie2 receptor levels in cell lines and murine critical limb ischemia models.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 exposure increased angiopoietin-2, Tie2 receptor, and VEGFA expression. These factors enable blood vessel formation, stabilization, and maturation. Similar protein increases observed in muscle tissue of ischemic mice following TB-500 administration.

**Notch and NF-κB Pathway Activation**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1437):** Investigation of signaling pathways mediating TB-500 pro-angiogenic effects; measurement via pathway blocking studies in cell lines and murine models.
	- **Findings:** Blocking Notch pathway or NF-κB pathway counteracted TB-500’s pro-angiogenic effects, establishing these pathways as critical mediators of TB-500-induced angiogenesis.

**Muscle and Tendon Injury Recovery**

**Accelerated Skeletal Muscle Regeneration and Scar Formation Reduction**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1431, 1432):** Animal models of muscle injury; TB-500 administration; assessment of muscle regeneration, fiber recovery, and scar formation reduction.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 accelerated skeletal muscle regeneration while reducing pathological scar formation in damaged muscle tissue.

**Tendon and Ligament Healing Enhancement**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1431, 1434):** Tendon and ligament injury models; TB-500 treatment; measurement of healing progression, functional recovery, and fibrosis prevention.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 enhanced tendon and ligament healing through reduced fibrosis and improved tissue remodeling, supporting restoration of connective tissue function.

**Inflammation Reduction and Immune Modulation**

**MicroRNA-146a Upregulation and TLR Pathway Suppression (PMID: cited in 1440)**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1440):** Investigation of TB-500 inflammatory modulation; measurement of miR-146a expression and TLR proinflammatory signaling.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 upregulated miR-146a, which suppressed TLR proinflammatory pathway by targeting IRAK1 and TRAF6. This modulation promoted oligodendrocyte progenitor cell (OPC) differentiation to mature myelin-producing oligodendrocytes.

**Pro-Inflammatory Cytokine Downregulation**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1432, 1434):** Measurement of pro-inflammatory cytokine levels (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6) following TB-500 administration; assessment of anti-inflammatory effects.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 downregulated pro-inflammatory cytokines while upregulating anti-inflammatory signals, creating favorable inflammatory environment for healing.

**Fibrosis Prevention and Anti-Adhesion Formation**

**Reduced Fibrosis and Scar Tissue Formation**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1429, 1431, 1437):** Assessment of fibrosis markers and tissue scarring following TB-500 treatment; comparison with untreated injury controls.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 significantly reduced pathological fibrosis and scar formation characteristic of inappropriate wound healing.

**Prevention of Muscle, Tendon, and Ligament Adhesions**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1429):** Investigation of TB-500 effects on adhesion formation in post-injury tissue.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 prevented formation of adhesions and fibrous bands characteristic of chronic muscle, tendon, and ligament injury sequelae.

**Actin Binding and Cytoskeletal Regulation**

**G-Actin Sequestration and Cell Migration Enhancement**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1429, 1432, 1437):** Investigation of TB-500 actin binding mechanism; measurement of G-actin sequestration and effects on cell cytoskeletal dynamics.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 bound globular actin (G-actin), sequestering it and modulating cytoskeletal dynamics to enable enhanced cellular migration.

**Cardiovascular and Ischemic Tissue Effects**

**Critical Limb Ischemia Model Improvement**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1437, 1441):** Murine models of critical limb ischemia; TB-500 administration; assessment of angiogenesis, tissue perfusion, and functional recovery.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 promoted vascular growth and improved tissue perfusion in ischemic limbs through enhanced angiogenesis.

**Endothelial Function Enhancement**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1432, 1437):** Measurement of endothelial cell differentiation and vascular function parameters following TB-500 exposure.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 enhanced endothelial cell differentiation and improved vascular function supporting tissue oxygen delivery.

**Neuropathic Complications Prevention**

**Peripheral Neuropathy Recovery in Type 2 Diabetes**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1440):** Type II diabetic mouse model of peripheral neuropathy; TB-500 administration; assessment of nerve function and myelination recovery.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 promoted recovery of peripheral neuropathy through microRNA-146a-mediated mechanisms and enhanced nerve remyelination.

**Matrix Remodeling and Collagen Synthesis**

**Matrix Metalloproteinase Induction and Extracellular Matrix Degradation**

- **Study (PMID: cited in 1429, 1435):** Investigation of TB-500 effects on matrix metalloproteinase (MMP) expression; measurement of collagen and ECM component turnover.
	- **Findings:** TB-500 increased MMP expression during wound repair phase, promoting appropriate extracellular matrix remodeling and collagen turnover.

**Note on Research Status and Clinical Development:** TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4 Fragment) represents a well-characterized investigational peptide with extensive preclinical research documenting tissue regeneration, wound healing, angiogenesis, and anti-inflammatory efficacy as of October 2025. The octapeptide has demonstrated 42-61% wound healing acceleration, 2-3-fold keratinocyte migration enhancement, potent muscle and tendon regeneration, fibrosis prevention, and angiogenic capacity through multiple molecular mechanisms. However, TB-500 has **NOT undergone FDA approval** and remains classified as a research compound available exclusively through specialized suppliers as of October 2025. Most published evidence derives from preclinical animal models with limited human clinical trial data. TB-500 remains available for investigational research purposes through peptide suppliers but has not advanced to pharmaceutical development stages.
