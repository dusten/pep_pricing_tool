## Notes

- This is what most vendors mean when they list "TB-500"
- The actin-binding domain of TB4; drives actin polymerization and cell migration
- Higher stability than full TB4: N-acetylation + small size → longer effective duration (cited 24–36h)
- More bioavailable and cheaper to synthesize than full TB4
- **MW distinction:** TB-500 Fragment (846.97 g/mol) vs TB-500 with aldehyde modification (889 Da) — the aldehyde form is less stable; most vendor stock is the fragment without it
- Extensive preclinical data for wound healing, muscle/tendon repair, angiogenesis
