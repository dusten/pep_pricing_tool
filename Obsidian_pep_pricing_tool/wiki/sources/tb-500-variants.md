# TB-500 Variants Comparison

**Origin:** `raw/clippings/dustenPep-Resources 1.md`
**Source URL:** https://github.com/dusten/Pep-Resources/blob/main/TB-500_Variants.md

## Key takeaways

- Three compounds share the "TB" label in vendor/community contexts — only one is the full natural peptide
- "TB-500" in vendor listings almost always = 7aa fragment (Thymosin B5 Acetate, CAS 885340-08-9), **not** full TB4
- TB5 (CAS 948841-07-4) is a small-molecule MAO-B inhibitor — not a peptide, not thymosin-derived; at least one vendor (Biosynth) has mislabeled it
- CAS number is the only reliable differentiator across all three
- Fragment preferred in research: cheaper, more stable, better bioavailability; narrower mechanism than full TB4
- Full TB4 has broader systemic effects but shorter half-life and higher cost
