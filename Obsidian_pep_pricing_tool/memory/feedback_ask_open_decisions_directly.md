When a spec or plan surfaces genuine open decisions (numeric thresholds, behavior choices between real alternatives), actively ask via AskUserQuestion in the same turn rather than just writing them into a doc's "Open Decisions" section and treating that as having surfaced them.

**Why:** Wrote the ZIP-upload spec ([[../wiki/analyses/2026-07-02-zip-upload-spec]]) with 4 flagged open decisions and stopped there. User came back with "Ask me the 4 questions because I have thoughts" — they wanted to be asked directly, not to have to separately notice and respond to a list buried in a document.

**How to apply:** Once a spec's open decisions are identified and it's genuinely the user's call (not something with an obvious right answer), ask them via AskUserQuestion right then, rather than parking the questions in prose and waiting to see if the user brings them up.
